🔍 Interested in Inspecting Our Work First Hand?

These are major investments. Before hiring any contractor for a roofing, siding, or window project, ask all 8 questions. We answer yes to every one.

✓
Is your Contractor licensed?
A valid state license proves the contractor meets minimum competency standards and is legally permitted to work in Illinois. Always ask to see the license number — ours is #104.014799.
✓
Is your Contractor bonded?
A surety bond protects you financially if the contractor fails to complete the job or causes damage. Bonding is a sign of professional accountability.
✓
Is your Contractor insured?
General liability and workers' compensation insurance protect you from being held responsible for on-site injuries or property damage. Never let an uninsured crew on your roof.
✓
Does your Contractor have an A+ rating with the BBB?
The Better Business Bureau A+ rating reflects a contractor's complaint history, responsiveness, and ethical practices. Countryside has maintained an A+ rating complaint-free since 2004.
✓
Have you researched your Contractor through EBSCO Research Best Picks Reports or Angie's List?
Independent research services like EBSCO and Angi verify contractor quality through real homeowner reviews and background checks. Countryside has been recognised by both.
✓
Did your Contractor provide you with a written estimate?
A written estimate locks in scope, materials, and pricing — protecting you from surprise charges. Verbal quotes leave you with no recourse if the story changes mid-project.
✓
Is your Contractor pulling a Building Permit from the Township?
Permits ensure the work is inspected and meets local code. Skipping permits can void your home insurance, create title issues, and result in costly corrections at resale.
✓
Is your Contractor local or from out of state?
Out-of-state storm chasers often disappear after payment. A local contractor has a permanent address, community reputation, and is here to honour your warranty for the long haul.
